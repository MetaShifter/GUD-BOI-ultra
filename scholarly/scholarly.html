Abstract

This report presents the GUDBOI application, which was created as a 2024 HCI Project at FII UAIC.

Introduction

In today's world, due to work and lack of time, dog lovers face several issues: inability to pay close attention to their pets, forgetting regular check-ups, morning rush causing feeding issues, lack of training knowledge etc.

The GUDBOI app proposes an innovative new method of pet management, including notifications, automatic feeding, health tracking and training courses, ensuring proper nutrition, tracking and increasing the overall life quality of users' beloved dogs.

1.1 App purpose

The purpose of the app is making pet ownership and home training easier and easing the added stress of surpise issues owners tend to encounter when missing the subtle signs their dogs are showing.

1.2 Target audience

The target audience consists of working dog owners who wish to keep track of their dogs' health(vet appointments, appetite), training, trick knowledge and feeding (portion sizes, feeding time) much easier.

2. Functionalities

2.1 Training

The training functionality allows the user to keep track of their dog's abilities and trick knowledge. For each of the training seessions, the app offers step by step tried and true training methods in order to teach the pet in the most efficient way. The app also offers a record option in order to be able to watch the pet's progress later on.

2.2 Feeding

The feeding functionality works based on user input of their dog's:

  • breed
  • weight
  • age
  • food type
  • feeding times
Based on these, the automatic feeder that the app connects to will dispense the recommended amount of food at the pre determined feeding times. The integrated scale will also be able to determine the amound of food the pet has eaten and keep records of that in the dog's profile.

2.3 Veterinary

This functionality will allow users to:

  • Keep track of regular vet check-ups
  • Receive notifications if their dog appetite is abnormal
  • Receive notifications in case a vet visit is recommended
  • Keep track of the dog's medical records
  • Keep track of the dog's prescriptions

3. Interaction design patterns

3.1 Wizard

The wizard design pattern refers to processes alowing users to input information. In this case, the steps represent the instructions that the user picks to train their dog. After the selection is made, the app offers specific information regarding the training: videos, progress, instructions.

3.2 Acheivements

This design pattern refers to engaging users and convincing them to use the app more in order to gather the recognition. In the GUD BOI app, this is done by tracking the evolution of each skill the dog has learned.

3.3 Need-for-closure

This pattern prompts users to use the app in order to be done with what seems like unfinished business. This is done by sending reminders for "training time" and for finishing a training course, as well as with the visual progression of each training in the trainingpage.

4. Design decisions

4.1 Mobile

Regarding the mobile design of the app, it allows the most freedom out of the 2 platforms where it is available.

This is the platform where most of the active interaction with the app is happening. The user is able to do multiple actions, such as:

  • Input data about their dog
  • Record the training sessions via the phone camera
  • Keep and see the medical records of the animal
  • Keep track of the dog's prescriptions
  • Record training sessions
  • View past training sessions

4.2 Smartwatch

Regarding the watch design of the app, it acts as a extension that makes the main mobile app easier to use. Due to the size of the screen, the icons and elements of the app have to be as simple as possible, to avoid accidental clicking of something other than intended. The commands consist of push buttons in order to be able to use them with ease even when in a rush. The only exceptions are the sliding screens for picking the functionality and for picking the training.

This platform allows the user to continue the phone actions without needing to keep the phone on hand at all times. These actions include:

  • Receiving notifications
  • Selecting the desired training session you want to start
  • Starting/stoping the training session
  • Sending treat thorugh the automatic feeder
  • Viewing automatic feeder live camera
  • Real time feedback about training command success
